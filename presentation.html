This is happening right now. When student data is pasted into public AI tools, it leaves the Fontys environment without consent, without agreements, and without any privacy protection. The Data We're Handling

Student Responses Contain Very Sensitive Information

Health & Disability

Students describing their own physical conditions or disabilities

"As someone with dyslexia, I find the reading load…"

Mental Health

Mentions of burnout, anxiety, depression, or study-related stress

"I've been struggling with anxiety this year and…"

Religion & Beliefs

References to religious background, practices, or obligations

"During Ramadan I found it difficult to attend…"

Discrimination

Experiences of discrimination based on ethnicity or background

"As an international student I often felt excluded…"
These categories carry the highest level of legal protection under European privacy law (GDPR Article 9) — they require special safeguards and cannot be processed without specific justification.

Privacy by Design

Small Groups Are Always Protected

Threshold: 10 students — confirmed direction from quality officers
The rule is simple:

If fewer than 10 students responded in a group, the system shows no results at all for that group — not even a partial answer.

This prevents anyone from piecing together who said what, even after personal details have been removed.

Infrastructure

Where the Data Can and Cannot Go

GDPR does not prohibit cloud use — but given the sensitive nature of this data, only one option is defensible without major legal complications.

Recommended
Fontys Servers
Data never leaves the Fontys network. All processing takes place on Fontys infrastructure. This is the only option with no additional legal complications.
Preferred approach
If needed
EU Cloud Provider
An EU-based provider with no American ownership. Allowed, but requires formal written agreements and explicit approval from the Fontys Privacy Officer.
Additional steps required
Not acceptable
American Cloud Services
Services like ChatGPT or OpenAI transfer data to American servers. Given the special categories of personal data involved, this creates legal risks that cannot be easily resolved.
Not for this project
GDPR Art. 44–49 (international data transfers) · Art. 28 (processor agreements)
